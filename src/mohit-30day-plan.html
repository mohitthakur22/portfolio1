10 Golden Rules — Read Every Morning
01
Build, don't just watchFor every 1 hour of video, spend 2 hours building something. Watching feels like learning. Building IS learning.
02
No AI for logic during practiceUse Codeium/Copilot at work. But during study sessions, write every line yourself. Your hands need to remember, not just your eyes.
03
Say answers out loud — alwaysReading an answer and saying it are completely different skills. Interviews require speaking. Practice speaking every single day.
04
Consistency beats intensity4 hours every day beats 12 hours one day then burnout. Show up every day even if tired. Even 1 hour is better than zero.
05
Never lie about what you knowIf you don't know something in the interview — say "I haven't worked with that directly but I understand the concept and I learn fast." That's honest and impressive.
06
Your story is your superpowerMechanical engineer → self-taught → employed at Wipro on HPE project. That story shows hunger. Own it with pride, not shame.
07
Apply in volume — track everythingKeep a spreadsheet: company, date applied, status. Apply to 3–5 jobs per week minimum from week 3 onwards. Interviews are a numbers game.
08
Learn from every interview you getAfter each interview — write down every question they asked. Study anything you couldn't answer. Each interview makes you better for the next one.
09
Sleep and rest are part of the planA tired brain learns nothing. 7 hours of sleep is not optional. The rest day on Sunday is not weakness — it's strategy.
10
Start tonight — not tomorrowOpen VS Code right now. Build something small. The hardest step is always the first one. You already know enough to start.
